About Us

Welcome to Wish Islands

A New Era of Luxury Across the Maldives

Wish Islands is a luxury hospitality brand born from the vision of our esteemed mother company, WCC – World Capital Centre. Rooted in innovation, elegance, and world-class service, Wish Islands represents the next generation of destination experiences across the Maldives and beyond.

As a proud subsidiary of WCC, Wish Islands embodies the group’s long-standing legacy in global development, capital investment, and visionary infrastructure. Our resorts, villas, and leisure properties reflect the same ambition and excellence that fueled WCC’s iconic ventures.

At Wish Islands, we bring paradise to life through sustainable design, curated luxury, and authentic Maldivian hospitality. With stunning island resorts such as Kaalhehutta, Villigaala, Bolimathaidoo, and Ukuruhutta, we are redefining what it means to retreat, recharge, and reconnect.

About Al Aman World Capital Centre Limited

Al Aman World Capital Centre Limited, commonly referred to as Al Aman, is a diversified conglomerate with a significant presence in various industries across multiple countries, including Sri Lanka, Switzerland, Canada, Singapore, the United Kingdom, and the United Arab Emirates.

The group’s business portfolio spans capital markets, engineering, real estate, hospitality, security, and apparel sectors.

One of Al Aman’s notable ventures was the proposed World Capital Centre (WCC) in Colombo, Sri Lanka. Announced in 2015, the WCC was envisioned as a 117-story twin-tower structure reaching a height of 625 meters. The project aimed to include 1,200 residential units, 2,000 hotel rooms, 5,000 car parking spaces, and 3,000 retail units, positioning it as a significant addition to Colombo’s skyline. The development was intended to be a 100% foreign direct investment, valued at approximately USD 2.4 billion.

The WCC was designed to feature 50 distinct zones catering to various industries, such as tourism, finance, and retail, facilitating streamlined access to related services within the complex. Amenities were to include multiple swimming pools, a seven-star hotel, and the first Michelin-starred restaurant in South Asia.

However, the project faced scrutiny regarding its feasibility and the credibility of the investors. Concerns were raised about the project’s scale, funding sources, and the experience of the developers in undertaking such a monumental endeavor. These factors led to debates about the project’s potential impact on Sri Lanka’s economy and infrastructure.
